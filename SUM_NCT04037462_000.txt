This study tests if a steroid called Dexamethasone, added to immunotherapy, helps your immune system fight non-small cell lung cancer that grew despite immunotherapy. You'll have scans, blood draws, and take the steroid before your first three treatments, over up to six years. Risks include infection, mood changes, and small radiation exposure; other risks may be unknown. Benefits aren't guaranteed, but you might respond better to treatment. Alternatives include other treatments, palliative care, or hospice. Your information stays confidential with a coded ID, and your samples won't be used in future studies. Study procedures are free, though normal co-payments still apply. The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) covers injury-related treatment, with no extra payment for injuries. You'll get $25 per scan visit. Joining is voluntary; withdraw anytime without losing benefits, and researchers may end participation if needed. You'll learn about new findings.
