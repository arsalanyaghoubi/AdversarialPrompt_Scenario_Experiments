**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Title:** Phase 1b/2 trial of preoperative niraparib, dostarlimab, and hypo-fractionated radiotherapy for the treatment of locally advanced rectal cancers

**Study Purpose:** This study aims to determine the best tolerated dose of niraparib when administered with both radiation and dostarlimab, and to identify the side effects of this combined therapy.

**Study Duration:** Up to 15 weeks, involving up to 12 weeks of treatment, with follow-up visits for 2 years after radiation therapy.

**Study Participants:** Up to 55 people with locally advanced rectal cancer will participate in this study.

**Study Involvement:**

1. Blood tests to determine blood counts, liver, and kidney function.
2. PET/CT or CT scan of the chest, abdomen, and pelvis.
3. Pelvic MRI.
4. Urinalysis.
5. Physical exam with medical history and vital signs.
6. Pregnancy test (if applicable).
7. Flexible sigmoidoscopy with biopsies (study week 8).
8. Follow-up appointments in UIHC-Radiation Oncology at 6 months, 12 months, and 2 years after completing radiation therapy.

**Risks and Side Effects:**

1. Common side effects: diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, fatigue, weakness, dizziness, and hair loss.
2. Rare but serious side effects: low blood cell counts, anemia, high blood pressure, allergic reactions, and posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome (PRES).
3. Potential risks: secondary primary malignancy, PARP inhibitor class effects, and radiation sensitization.

**Benefits:**

1. Potential improved effectiveness of treatment.
2. Contribution to future research and development of new treatments.

**Confidentiality and Data Storage:**

1. Protected health information will be used for research purposes.
2. Data will be stored with a code linked to the participant's name, date of birth, and medical record number.
3. Imaging, data, and tumor tissue may be stored for future research.

**Informed Consent:**

1. Participants must provide informed consent before participating in the study.
2. Participants have the right to withdraw from the study at any time.
3. Participants will be informed of any changes to the study protocol.
