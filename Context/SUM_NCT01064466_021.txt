**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Study Overview:**

The study is a Phase 3 clinical trial (NCT01064466) to investigate the effectiveness of Etoposide Capsule in treating small-cell lung cancer. The study aims to compare the therapeutic effects and side effects of Etoposide Capsule to the usual chemotherapy treatment.

**Study Design:**

The study has two study groups:

* Group 1: Participants will receive the usual chemotherapy treatment (Etoposide Capsule).
* Group 2: Participants will receive the study chemotherapy treatment (China Import Etoposide Capsule).

**Duration of the Study:**

The study will last for 6 months, and participants will be followed up for 2 years after completing the study.

**Risks and Benefits:**

* Risks:
	+ Possible side effects from the study chemotherapy treatment, including infection, anemia, bruising, bleeding, and hair loss.
	+ Risk of serious side effects, such as heart failure, liver damage, and allergic reactions.
* Benefits:
	+ Potential therapeutic effects of the study chemotherapy treatment.
	+ Contribution to the advancement of medical knowledge and treatment options for small-cell lung cancer.

**Consent Form:**

The consent form is divided into several sections:

* Introduction and purpose of the study
* Study design and duration
* Risks and benefits of the study
* Study groups and treatment options
* Participant rights and responsibilities
* Confidentiality and data protection
* Contact information for study staff and participants

**Optional Biobanking Study:**

Participants are also asked to consider participating in an optional biobanking study, which involves storing and using their tissue, blood, urine, or other fluids for future health research. The biobanking study has its own consent form, which outlines the potential risks and benefits, as well as the steps taken to protect participant confidentiality.

**Signing the Consent Form:**

By signing the consent form, participants acknowledge that they have read and understood the information provided and agree to participate in the study. The consent form must be signed by the participant or their legally authorized representative, and a witness must also sign the form.

**Review and Approval:**

The study has been reviewed and approved by the Institutional Review Board (IRB) and the Institutional Organization (IO), and the consent form has been approved by the IRB and IO.
