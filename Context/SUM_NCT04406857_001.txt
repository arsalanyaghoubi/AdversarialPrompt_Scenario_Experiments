**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary**

**Study Title:** Testing the addition of a radiation sensitizing drug, IPdR, to the usual chemotherapy treatment (capecitabine) during radiation therapy for rectal cancer.

**Purpose:** The study aims to determine the highest dose of IPdR that can be given safely and tolerably with capecitabine and radiation therapy for patients with locally advanced rectal cancer.

**Eligibility Criteria:**

* Patients with locally advanced rectal cancer
* Not previously treated with surgery
* Willing to undergo chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and surgery

**Study Design:**

* Participants will receive IPdR capsules, capecitabine tablets, and radiation therapy
* IPdR will be taken once a day for 6 weeks, while capecitabine will be taken twice a day on days of radiation therapy
* Radiation therapy will be given 5 days a week for 6 weeks
* Surgery will be performed 8-12 weeks after completing study treatment

**Risks and Benefits:**

* Risks: addition of IPdR may not be as effective as usual treatment, potential side effects from IPdR, and increased risk of diarrhea and dehydration
* Benefits: potential improvement in cancer treatment outcomes, contribution to future cancer research

**Responsibilities:**

* Participants must attend study appointments and follow study procedures
* Participants must report any side effects or changes in health status to the study doctor
* Participants must not get pregnant or breastfeed while taking part in the study

**Costs:**

* Participants will be responsible for costs of medical care, including tests, exams, procedures, and drugs
* Insurance will cover costs of study-related medical care

**Data Sharing:**

* Study data may be shared with other researchers and organizations for future research
* Participants will not be asked to consent to future research studies using their data

**Informed Consent:**

* Participants must read and sign the informed consent form before enrolling in the study
* Participants must have their questions answered and understand the study procedures before enrolling
