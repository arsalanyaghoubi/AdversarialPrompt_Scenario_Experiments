This clinical trial consent form describes a Phase I/II trial of a new combination therapy consisting of PLX038 (PEGylated SN38) and Rucaparib in patients with solid tumors and small cell cancers. 

**Key Information:**

* The trial is being conducted at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) Clinical Center.
* The purpose of the study is to find a safe combination of PLX038 and Rucaparib and to see if this combination will cause tumors to shrink.
* PLX038 is considered investigational and has not been approved by the FDA to treat cancer. Rucaparib has been approved for use in some cancers, but not for small cell cancers.
* The study involves administering PLX038 intravenously on Day 1 of each cycle (21 days) and Rucaparib orally on Days 6-19 of each cycle.
* Patients will be required to undergo regular physical exams, blood tests, imaging studies, and other procedures to monitor their health and response to treatment.
* The study may benefit patients by shrinking their tumors or lessening their symptoms.
* Patients may experience side effects, which can be mild, serious, temporary, or permanent.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* The study drugs may affect various parts of the body, including the liver, kidneys, heart, and blood.
* Common side effects of Rucaparib include nausea, fatigue, low blood counts, and changes in kidney and liver function blood tests.
* Less frequent side effects of Rucaparib include myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and acute myeloid leukemia (AML).
* Rare, serious side effects of Rucaparib include allergic reactions, which can be life-threatening.

**Other Risks:**

* The study involves radiation exposure from CT scans, which may increase the risk of cancer.
* There is a risk of genetic testing, which may reveal genetic risks for other diseases or disabilities.
* The study may involve the collection and storage of biological specimens, which may be used for future research.
* There is a risk of breach of confidentiality, although the NIH will take steps to protect patient information.

**Benefits:**

* Patients may benefit from the study by experiencing tumor shrinkage or symptom relief.
* The study may provide valuable information for future research and treatment of cancer.

**Consent:**

* Patients are free to withdraw from the study at any time.
* Patients will be asked to provide written consent before participating in the study.
* Patients will be informed of any new information that may affect their decision to participate in the study.

**Confidentiality:**

* The NIH will take steps to protect patient information and maintain confidentiality.
* Patient information will be stored and used for research purposes, but will not be disclosed without consent.

**Contact Information:**

* Patients can contact the Principal Investigator, Anish Thomas, M.D., at anish.thomas@nih.gov or 240-760-7343 with questions or concerns.

Overall, this clinical trial consent form provides a comprehensive overview of the study, including its purpose, risks, benefits, and procedures. Patients will be required to provide written consent before participating in the study, and will be informed of any new information that may affect their decision to participate.
