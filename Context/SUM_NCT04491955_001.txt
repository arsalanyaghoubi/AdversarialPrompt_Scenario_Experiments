**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Study Title:** Phase II Trial of Combination Immunotherapy in Subjects with Advanced Small Bowel and Colorectal Cancers

**Objective:** To determine if a new combination of immunotherapy drugs is effective in shrinking tumors in participants with advanced small bowel and colorectal cancers.

**Key Information:**

* Participants will receive a combination of CV301 vaccines (MVA-BN-CV301 and FPV-CV301), M7824, N-803, and NHS-IL12.
* The treatment will last for approximately one year, with regular follow-up visits and tests to monitor side effects and disease progression.
* Participants may experience side effects, including fatigue, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, chills, anemia, and bleeding.
* The study will involve the collection of blood and tumor samples for research purposes.
* Participants will be followed for long-term follow-up, including phone or email contact every 3 months for one year and then every 6 months after the first year.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Common side effects of CV301 vaccines include flu-like symptoms, muscle or joint ache, tiredness, headache, nausea, and changes in appetite.
* Common side effects of M7824 include fatigue, diarrhea, nausea, constipation, swelling of the lower legs or hands, vomiting, fever, loss of body fluids, decreased appetite, skin growths, rash, blisters, skin discoloration, and other skin abnormalities.
* Common side effects of N-803 include fast heart rate, dry eyes, nausea, vomiting, fever, fatigue, abnormal liver enzymes, flushing, itching rash, stomach pain, joint pain, headache, blood in the urine, muscle pains, altered taste, redness, soreness, or swelling at the injection site.
* Common side effects of NHS-IL12 include decreased white blood cells, increased liver enzymes, hyperglycemia, anemia, flu-like symptoms, fever, increased alkaline phosphatase, fatigue, thrombocytopenia, hypoalbuminemia, and hypophosphatemia.

**Other Risks:**

* Risks associated with blood collection, including pain and bruising, lightheadedness, and fainting.
* Risks associated with biopsy, including infection or bleeding.
* Risks associated with CT scans, including exposure to radiation.
* Risks associated with MRI scans, including exposure to radiation and potential allergic reactions.
* Risks associated with gadolinium contrast, including allergic reactions and kidney damage.

**Benefits:**

* Potential reduction in tumor bulk, which may have a favorable impact on symptoms and/or survival.
* Contribution to future research and development of new treatments for small bowel and colorectal cancers.

**Confidentiality and Privacy:**

* The NIH will take steps to protect participant confidentiality and privacy, including the use of a Certificate of Confidentiality and the Privacy Act.
* Participants will not be identified in any public reports or publications.
* Researchers will not release identifiable information without participant consent.

**Compensation and Reimbursement:**

* Participants will not receive compensation for participation in the study.
* The NIH will cover the cost of some expenses, including travel and lodging.
* Participants may be reimbursed for expenses not covered by the NIH.

**Informed Consent:**

* Participants will be fully informed about the study and its risks and benefits.
* Participants will be given the opportunity to ask questions and discuss the study with the research team.
* Participants will be required to provide informed consent before participating in the study.

**Withdrawal from the Study:**

* Participants may withdraw from the study at any time.
* Participants will be informed of the reason for withdrawal and will be provided with information about their rights as a research participant.
