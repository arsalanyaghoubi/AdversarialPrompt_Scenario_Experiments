**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Title:** Testing the ability of Pembrolizumab to alter the Tumor Immune MicroEnvironment (TIME) of high risk DCIS (ductal carcinoma in situ).

**Purpose:** The study aims to investigate the effects of Pembrolizumab, a cancer treatment drug, on the immune system's response to high-risk DCIS. The study will also examine whether intralesional injection of Pembrolizumab can alter the tumor's immune microenvironment.

**Number of Participants:** Approximately 39 patients will participate in the study.

**Duration:** The study will last for about 8-15 weeks.

**Inclusion Criteria:** Patients with high-risk DCIS are eligible to participate in the study.

**Exclusion Criteria:** Patients who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a history of certain medical conditions are not eligible to participate in the study.

**Study Procedures:**

1. **Screening:** Patients will undergo a series of exams, tests, and procedures to determine their eligibility for the study.
2. **Treatment:** Patients will receive intralesional injections of Pembrolizumab at 3-week intervals for a total of 4 doses.
3. **Surgery:** Patients will undergo surgery to remove the tumor, which will be performed 3 weeks after the final dose of Pembrolizumab.
4. **Follow-up:** Patients will undergo follow-up exams and tests to monitor their response to treatment and identify any potential side effects.

**Potential Benefits:** Participation in the study may help doctors and scientists learn more about the immune system's response to high-risk DCIS and develop new treatments for cancer.

**Potential Risks:** Patients may experience side effects from the study drug, including itching, cough, loose stools, joint pain, fever, rash, back pain, and loss of skin color. Rare but serious side effects may include inflammation of the lungs, pancreas, or eyes, and low blood pressure.

**Privacy Risks:** Patients' personal information and medical records may be shared with researchers, healthcare providers, and insurance companies.

**Compensation:** Patients will not receive payment for participating in the study.

**Withdrawal:** Patients can withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

**Contact Information:** Patients can contact the study doctors, Dr. Laura Esserman and Dr. Jasmine Wong, or the Office of the Committee on Human Research at 415-476-1814 with questions or concerns.

**Additional Information:** Patients can visit the ClinicalTrials.gov website to learn more about the study and its results.
