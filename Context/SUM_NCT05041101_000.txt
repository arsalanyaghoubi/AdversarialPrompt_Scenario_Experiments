**Summary of Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Study Name:** Phase Ib/II Study of Grapiprant (IK-007) and Eribulin Combination Treatment for Metastatic Inflammatory Breast Cancer (mIBC)

**Objective:** To evaluate the safety and effectiveness of combining grapiprant (IK-007) and eribulin in treating metastatic inflammatory breast cancer (mIBC).

**Eligibility:** Patients with mIBC who are 18 years or older, have a medical record number, and are able to provide informed consent.

**Study Design:**

1. **Screening Tests:** Participants will undergo physical exams, EKGs, MUGA scans or echocardiograms, blood draws, urine collection, imaging scans, and biopsies to assess their eligibility for the study.
2. **Study Groups:** Participants will be enrolled in one of two study groups (Group 1 or Group 2) based on when they enroll in the study.
3. **Study Drug Administration:** Participants will receive eribulin by vein on Days 1 and 8 of each 21-day cycle. Grapiprant will be taken orally twice daily with food or within 2 hours after eating.
4. **Study Visits:** Participants will have regular study visits, including physical exams, blood draws, EKGs, and imaging scans.
5. **Follow-Up:** Participants will be followed up for up to 5 years after their last dose of study drugs.

**Possible Risks:**

1. **Side Effects:** Participants may experience side effects, including high blood pressure, fast/irregular heartbeat, abdominal pain, vomiting, inflammation of the intestines, stomach ulcers, diarrhea, abnormal liver tests, abnormal kidney tests, and others.
2. **Rare but Serious Side Effects:** Participants may experience rare but serious side effects, including abnormal EKG, low blood levels of magnesium, inflammation of the pancreas, dehydration, low platelet count, liver damage, lung inflammation, severe life-threatening infection, and allergic reaction.

**Other Risks:**

1. **Blood Draws:** Participants may experience pain, bleeding, and/or bruising from blood draws.
2. **Biopsies:** Participants may experience pain, bruising, bleeding, redness, low blood pressure, swelling, and/or infection from biopsies.
3. **Imaging Scans:** Participants may experience discomfort, skin irritation, and/or allergic reactions from imaging scans.

**Pregnancy-Related Risks:**

1. **Participation:** Pregnant or breastfeeding participants will not be enrolled in the study.
2. **Birth Control:** Participants who can become pregnant or father a child must use birth control during the study and at least 6 months after their last dose of study drugs.

**Costs and Compensation:**

1. **Medical Care:** MD Anderson will provide medical care if participants suffer injury as a direct result of taking part in the study.
2. **Reimbursement:** Participants may receive reimbursement for expenses, including travel expenses, up to a maximum of $600.

**Future Research:**

1. **Data:** Participants' personal information may be used for future research.
2. **Samples:** Samples collected from participants may be used for future research, including genetic research.
3. **Genetic Research:** Participants' samples may be used for genetic research, including whole genome sequencing.

**Authorization for Use and Disclosure of Protected Health Information (PHI):**

1. **Use of PHI:** Participants' PHI will be used for legal, ethical, research, and safety-related reasons.
2. **Disclosure of PHI:** Participants' PHI may be shared with federal agencies, the IRB, IKENA Oncology, Labcorp, and others.

**Consent and Authorization:**

1. **Optional:** Participants may choose not to take part in the study or withdraw from participation at any time.
2. **Confidentiality:** MD Anderson will keep participants' PHI confidential when possible.

**Withdrawal of Authorization:**

1. **Written Request:** Participants may withdraw their authorization in writing.
2. **Removal from Study:** Participants will be removed from the study if they withdraw their authorization.

**Clinical Trial Summary:**

This clinical trial is a Phase Ib/II study to evaluate the safety and effectiveness of combining grapiprant (IK-007) and eribulin in treating metastatic inflammatory breast cancer (mIBC). Participants will undergo regular study visits, including physical exams, blood draws, EKGs, and imaging scans. Participants may experience side effects, including high blood pressure, fast/irregular heartbeat, abdominal pain, vomiting, inflammation of the intestines, stomach ulcers, diarrhea, abnormal liver tests, abnormal kidney tests, and others. Participants' PHI may be used for future research, including genetic research. Participants may choose not to take part in the study or withdraw from participation at any time.
