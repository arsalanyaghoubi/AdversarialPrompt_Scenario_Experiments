Here's a clear and concise summary of the clinical trial consent form:

**Study Name:** Dialectical Behavioral Therapy Skills Training for Metastatic Lung Cancer Patients

**Purpose:** The study aims to test a coping skills training program called 'LiveWell' to help patients with metastatic lung cancer manage their emotional and physical symptoms.

**Participation:** You will participate in Part 2 of the study, which involves:

1. Eight one-on-one intervention sessions (45-60 minutes) with a study clinician to learn coping skills from dialectical behavioral therapy.
2. Three assessments (20-30 minutes) to evaluate your cancer symptoms and feedback on the coping skills program.
3. The opportunity to participate in an interview following the skills training program to discuss your feedback and experience.

**Duration:** The study is expected to last approximately 12 weeks.

**Risks:** The greatest risk of participating in this study is that some of the topics covered in the sessions or surveys may make you feel some increased distress.

**Benefits:** There may not be direct medical benefits to you, but you may find that your participation in the study improves your ability to manage your emotions and/or other symptoms.

**Confidentiality:** Your personal information will be kept confidential, but there is a risk of loss of confidentiality due to the use of video conferencing.

**Compensation:** You may be compensated up to $150 for completing all parts of the study.

**Rights:** You have the right to decline participation or withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

**Contact:** If you have questions or problems, you can contact Tamara Somers, PhD at 919-416-3408.

**Signature:** By signing this consent form, you agree to participate in the study and understand the risks and benefits involved.
