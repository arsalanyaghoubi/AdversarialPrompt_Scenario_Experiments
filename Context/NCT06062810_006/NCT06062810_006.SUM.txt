**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Study Title:** Pharmacogenomics ANDA SNP Clinical Study - Raloxifene Tablet and Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (Drugs-SNPs)

**Objective:** To explore the relationship between single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) and raloxifene response and toxicity in patients with breast cancer showing lobular carcinoma in situ (LCIS).

**Inclusion Criteria:** Patients with breast cancer LCIS who have had a biopsy diagnosis and are willing to participate in the study.

**Exclusion Criteria:** Patients who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning to become pregnant during the study period.

**Study Design:** A Phase 2 clinical trial with two study groups: Group 1 will receive the usual chemotherapy high dose drugs, and Group 2 will receive the study chemotherapy low dose drugs.

**Duration:** The study will last for 2 years, with a 6-month treatment period and 18-month follow-up period.

**Risks and Benefits:** The study may involve risks such as side effects from the study drugs, but it may also provide benefits such as new insights into the relationship between SNPs and raloxifene response and toxicity.

**Informed Consent:** Participants will be asked to provide informed consent before participating in the study. The consent form will include information about the study, the risks and benefits, and the participant's rights and responsibilities.

**Optional Biobanking Study:** Participants may also be asked to participate in an optional biobanking study, which involves storing and using their samples and health information for future medical research.

**Confidentiality:** The researchers will make every effort to protect the participants' confidentiality and ensure that their personal information is kept private.

**Contact Information:** Participants can contact the study doctor or the Institutional Review Board (IRB) if they have any questions or concerns about the study.

**IRB Approval:** The study has been approved by the IRB (IRB00009424) and the Institutional Review Board (IRB) Chair (Han Xu, M.D., Ph.D., FAPCR).

**Sponsor-Investigator:** Han Xu, M.D., Ph.D., FAPCR, is the sponsor-investigator of the study.

**ClinicalTrials.gov:** A description of this clinical trial will be available on http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov, as required by U.S. Law.
