**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Purpose:** This study aims to evaluate the effectiveness of a new PET scan, called 68 Ga-PSMA-11, in detecting prostate cancer and guiding surgical treatment. The study will compare the results of the PET scan with post-surgical tissue analysis.

**Study Procedures:**

* Participants will undergo a single intravenous injection of the 68 Ga-PSMA-11 radiopharmaceutical and a PET/CT scan.
* Participants will be asked to complete baseline quality of life assessments, including erectile function, urinary function, and overall mental and physical health.
* After surgery, participants will continue to follow up with their physician and complete quality of life assessments at 1, 3, 6, and 12 months post-surgery.

**Risks:**

* Loss of confidentiality
* Radiopharmaceutical administration and PET/MR risks (e.g., allergic reactions, bruising, infection)
* Exposure to radiation, which may increase the risk of cancer and/or leukemia (although the risk is considered low)
* Unknown or unforeseeable risks associated with an investigational study
* Discomfort or uncomfortableness answering quality of life assessments

**Benefits:**

* The 68 Ga-PSMA-11 scan may detect the location, extent, and characteristics of prostate cancer, allowing for improved planning of surgery.
* Participants may experience improved quality of life due to more accurate surgical planning.

**Alternatives:**

* Participants may choose not to participate in the study and continue with regular cancer treatment, including standard imaging scans.

**Confidentiality:**

* Every effort will be made to keep personal information confidential, but absolute confidentiality cannot be guaranteed.
* Personal information may be shared outside the research study if required by law or to individuals or organizations that oversee the conduct of research studies.

**Voluntary Nature of Study:**

* Participation in the study is voluntary, and participants may choose not to participate or leave the study at any time.
* Leaving the study will not result in any penalty or loss of benefits to which participants are entitled.
