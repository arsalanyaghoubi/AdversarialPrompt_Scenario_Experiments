**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Study Title:** Phase I/II Trial of HPV Vaccine PRGN-2009 Alone or in Combination with Anti-PD-L1/TGF-β trap (M7824) in Subjects with HPV Associated Cancers

**Objective:** The purpose of this study is to evaluate the safety and efficacy of the HPV vaccine PRGN-2009 alone or in combination with anti-PD-L1/TGF-β trap (M7824) in patients with HPV-associated cancers.

**Eligibility:** Patients with newly diagnosed stage I (T1,2 N1)/II/III p16-positive oropharyngeal cancer or operable stage II/III/IVA/IVB HPV+ sinonasal squamous cell cancer are eligible for this study.

**Study Design:** This is a Phase I/II trial with two phases: Phase I (investigating the safety and tolerability of the HPV vaccine) and Phase II (evaluating the efficacy of the HPV vaccine).

**Treatment:** Patients will receive the HPV vaccine PRGN-2009 alone or in combination with anti-PD-L1/TGF-β trap (M7824). The treatment will consist of two injections of the HPV vaccine on Day 1 and Day 15, followed by regular follow-up visits.

**Risks and Discomforts:** Possible side effects of the HPV vaccine include injection site reaction, fever, flu-like symptoms, poor appetite, increase in blood sugar, and skin rash. Additionally, patients may experience delays in standard therapy, which may result in a reduced chance of being cured from cancer.

**Benefits:** The potential benefits of this study include a reduction in the size of the tumor, which may have a favorable impact on symptoms and/or survival.

**Long-term Follow-up:** Patients will be followed for 5 years after completion of their surgery or chemoradiotherapy.

**Storage and Sharing of Specimens and Data:** The NIH will store and use the specimens and data from this study for future research purposes, including studies that may be conducted at other institutions.

**Confidentiality Protections:** The NIH will take precautions to protect the confidentiality of patients' information, including using a Certificate of Confidentiality and the Privacy Act.

**Contact Information:** Patients can contact the Principal Investigator, Charalampos Floudas, MD, at charalampos.floudas@nih.gov or 240-858-3032 with any questions or concerns about the study.

**Consent:** Patients must provide informed consent before participating in this study.
