**Clinical Trial Summary:**

**Title:** Vaccination of Triple Negative Breast Cancer Patients

**Purpose:** This clinical trial aims to compare the effectiveness of a new experimental breast cancer vaccine (P10s-PADRE/MONTANIDE ISA 51 VG) combined with standard chemotherapy and surgery to standard chemotherapy and surgery alone in treating triple negative breast cancer.

**Eligibility:** Women aged 18 and older with clinical stage I, II, or III triple negative breast cancer will be eligible for this study.

**Design:** This is a combined Phase I/II efficacy study with two treatment groups:

1. Group 1: Standard of care treatment consisting of chemotherapy and surgery.
2. Group 2: Experimental vaccine (P10s-PADRE/MONTANIDE ISA 51 VG) in addition to standard chemotherapy and surgery.

**Number of Participants:** Up to 102 women will be enrolled in this study, with 34 participants in Group 1 and 68 participants in Group 2.

**Duration:** The study will last approximately 70 weeks (about 1 year and 3 months).

**Involvement:** Participants will undergo standard medical care and additional research-related procedures, including:

* Blood work and urine tests
* Medical history and physical exams
* Skin tests
* Chemotherapy and surgery (standard care)
* Investigational vaccine (Group 2 only)
* Blood samples for research (up to 8 samples for Group 2, 3 samples for Group 1)

**Risks and Side Effects:** Common side effects may include localized itching, redness, and swelling at the injection site, decrease in energy, and temporary swelling of lymph nodes. Rare but serious side effects may include severe allergic reactions, overactivation of the immune system, and Guillain-Barre Syndrome.

**Benefits:** Participating in this study may help doctors learn more about breast cancer treatment and may contribute to the development of a breast cancer vaccine.

**Confidentiality:** Personal information will be kept confidential, but participants may be identified by study numbers on research documents.

**Withdrawal:** Participants can withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

**Future Use:** Participants may agree to have their leftover blood samples stored for future research studies on cancer vaccines.

**Genetic Studies:** Participants may agree to have their blood and tumor samples tested for genetic and epigenetic information to examine the effect of the vaccine on their immune system and tissue.

**Breast Tissue Samples:** Participants may agree to have their leftover breast tissue samples stored for future cancer vaccine research.

**Future Contact:** Participants may agree to be contacted for future research related to this study.
