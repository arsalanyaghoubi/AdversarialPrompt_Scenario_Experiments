**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Title:** A Pilot Study of Neoadjuvant High Dose Vitamin A for Resectable Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

**Purpose:** The study aims to investigate whether taking high-dose vitamin A (retinyl palmitate or retinyl palmitate/vitamin A) increases the number of germinal centers in tumors and lymph tissues, which may help improve the immune system's ability to fight lung cancer.

**Eligibility:** Participants must have known or suspected non-small cell lung cancer and be scheduled for surgery.

**Study Design:** Participants will be randomly assigned to either the vitamin A group or the control group. Those in the vitamin A group will take the supplement for 7 days before surgery, while those in the control group will not take the supplement. All participants will undergo surgery as part of their standard care.

**Risks and Benefits:**

* Risks: Taking high-dose vitamin A may cause side effects such as bleeding gums, sore mouth, bone pain, seizures, dry lips and mouth, diarrhea, double vision, and headache. There is also a risk of bleeding and infection from blood draws.
* Benefits: The study may provide valuable information about the potential benefits of vitamin A in treating lung cancer, but it is unlikely that individual participants will benefit directly from the study.

**Duration:** Participants will be in the study for approximately 2-3.5 months, including the 7-day vitamin A treatment period and follow-up visits after surgery.

**Confidentiality:** The study will maintain confidentiality of participant information, but there is a risk of a breach of confidentiality.

**Payment:** Participants will not receive payment for participating in the study, but their parking will be validated for study-related visits.

**Rights as a Research Participant:**

* Participants have the right to refuse participation or leave the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.
* Participants have the right to ask questions or raise concerns about the study or their rights as a research participant.
* The Institutional Review Board (IRB) will review the study to protect participant rights.
