**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Project Title:** Evaluation of a 68 Ga Small Molecule PSMA Antagonist Produced by Two Different Methods

**Objective:** To assess whether the PET/CT imaging results from two different production methods of 68 Ga PSMA are equivalent.

**Eligibility:** Patients with metastatic prostate cancer who are undergoing standard of care procedures.

**Involvement:**

* Two whole-body dynamic 68 Ga-PSMA PET/CT scans with a 6-48h gap
* Blood draws before, during, and after each PET/CT scan
* Serum blood draws for pharmacokinetic analysis (optional)
* Standard of care procedures, including medical history, physical exam, and laboratory tests

**Duration:** Up to 4 weeks

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Discomfort from needle insertions
* Exposure to radiation
* Low risk of allergic reaction from the radiotracer
* Unsuspected incidental findings that may lead to further medical work-up
* Potential costs for follow-up care and hospitalization

**Benefits:**

* No direct benefits to the participant
* Potential benefits to future patients with metastatic prostate cancer

**Confidentiality:**

* Efforts will be made to protect medical records and personal information
* Records may be shared with researchers, government agencies, and study sponsors

**Compensation:**

* No monetary compensation will be provided

**Rights as a Participant:**

* Voluntary participation
* Right to withdraw from the study at any time
* Right to access research records
* Right to cancel permission for researchers to use or share protected health information
