**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary**

**Study Information:**

* Protocol: Phase II Trial of Bevacizumab and Rucaparib in Recurrent Carcinoma of the Cervix or Endometrium
* Sponsor: University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center (OUHSC)
* Funding Collaborator: Clovis Oncology

**Purpose:**

The purpose of this study is to evaluate the effectiveness and safety of a combination treatment of rucaparib and bevacizumab in patients with recurrent cervical or endometrial cancer.

**Inclusion Criteria:**

* Patients with recurrent cervical or endometrial cancer
* Patients who have not received previous treatment with rucaparib and bevacizumab

**Exclusion Criteria:**

* Patients who are pregnant or breastfeeding
* Patients with a history of severe allergic reactions to rucaparib or bevacizumab
* Patients with a history of certain medical conditions, such as bleeding disorders or kidney disease

**Study Procedures:**

* Patients will undergo a series of tests and procedures, including blood work, CT scans, and biopsies
* Patients will receive treatment with rucaparib and bevacizumab for a maximum of 6 cycles
* Patients will be followed for up to 5 years after treatment completion

**Risks and Side Effects:**

* Common side effects of rucaparib include nausea, fatigue, low blood counts, and changes in kidney and liver function
* Common side effects of bevacizumab include high blood pressure, low appetite, nausea, vomiting, constipation or diarrhea
* Rare but serious side effects of rucaparib and bevacizumab include myelodysplastic syndrome, acute myeloid leukemia, and allergic reactions

**Benefits:**

* Patients may benefit from the treatment of rucaparib and bevacizumab, which may help to slow the growth of their cancer
* Patients will be contributing to the advancement of medical knowledge and the development of new treatments for cervical and endometrial cancer

**Confidentiality:**

* Efforts will be made to keep patient information confidential
* Patient specimens will be labeled with a unique code and will not be identifiable by name or description

**Consent:**

* Patients must provide informed consent before participating in the study
* Patients have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits

**Contact Information:**

* Patients can contact the study investigator or local IRB contact for questions or concerns about the study.
