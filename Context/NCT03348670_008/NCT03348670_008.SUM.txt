**Summary of Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

The clinical trial, NCT03348670, is a Phase 2 study that aims to explore the relationship between single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) and the response and toxicity of abiraterone in patients with prostate cancer. The study involves two groups: Group 1 will receive the usual chemotherapy high-dose drugs, while Group 2 will receive the study chemotherapy low-dose drugs.

**Key Information:**

* The study is exempt from the IND regulations and will be conducted in compliance with the requirements for institutional review (21 CFR Part 56) and informed consent (21 CFR Part 50).
* Participants will be randomly assigned to either Group 1 or Group 2.
* The study will last for 6 months, and participants will receive follow-up examinations for 2 years.
* Participants will undergo blood and urine tests, including sequencing of target genes and correlation of SNPs to therapeutic effects and side effects.
* The study will assess the efficacy and safety of the study chemotherapy drugs compared to the usual chemotherapy drugs.
* Participants can stop taking part in the study at any time.

**Risks and Benefits:**

* Risks: participants may experience side effects, and the study drugs may affect how different parts of the body work.
* Benefits: the study may help researchers learn things that will help people in the future, and participants may benefit from the study chemotherapy drugs.

**Confidentiality and Privacy:**

* Participants' medical information will be kept confidential, and researchers will make every effort to protect their identity.
* Participants' samples and related information may be stored in a biobank for use in future health research.

**Optional Biobanking Study:**

* Participants can choose to take part in an optional biobanking study, which involves storing and using their samples and health information for medical research.
* Participants will not benefit directly from the biobanking study, but their samples may contribute to future discoveries that could help people.
