**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Title:** Testing the Addition of a New Anti-Cancer Drug, Niraparib, to the Usual Treatment (Hormone and Radiation Therapy) for Prostate Cancer With a High Chance Of Recurring.

**Objective:** To determine if adding the new anti-cancer drug Niraparib to the usual treatment for prostate cancer with a high chance of recurring can lower the risk of the cancer growing or returning.

**Inclusion Criteria:** Patients with prostate cancer that has a high chance of recurring.

**Exclusion Criteria:** Patients with certain medical conditions or taking certain medications.

**Study Design:** Randomized Phase II Trial with an initial Phase I dose-finding portion.

**Treatment:** Patients will receive the usual treatment for prostate cancer (hormone therapy and radiation therapy) plus the experimental drug Niraparib.

**Duration:** Treatment will last for up to 24 months, with follow-up appointments every 6 months for 3 years after treatment.

**Risks and Benefits:**

* Risks: Side effects from the study treatment, including fatigue, low blood counts, hot flashes, high blood pressure, and erectile dysfunction. There is also a risk of developing new cancers, such as myelodysplastic syndrome and acute myeloid leukemia.
* Benefits: Potential benefit of adding Niraparib to the usual treatment for prostate cancer, although this is still unknown.

**Informed Consent:**

* Patients have the right to participate or not participate in the study.
* Patients can change their mind at any time.
* Patients will be informed about the risks and benefits of the study and will have the opportunity to ask questions.
* Patients will be told about the study's purpose, procedures, and potential risks and benefits.

**Optional Studies:**

* Patients can choose to participate in an optional study that involves collecting and storing tissue, blood, and urine samples for future research.
* Patients will not benefit from participating in this study, but the researchers hope that the results will help other people with cancer in the future.
* Patients can choose to participate or not participate in this study.

**Confidentiality:**

* Patients' personal information will be kept confidential.
* Researchers will not know who the patients are, and they will not try to find out.
* Personal information will not be given to anyone unless it is required by law.

**Payment:**

* Patients will not be paid for participating in the study.
* The research may lead to new tests, drugs, or other products for sale, but patients will not receive any payment.
