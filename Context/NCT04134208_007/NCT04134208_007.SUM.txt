**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary**

**Study Name:** 18 F-fluciclovine PET-CT as an indicator of therapeutic response in metastatic prostate carcinoma (M1PCa) 2019-0030

**Study Goal:** To determine if a radioactive imaging agent called 18 F-fluciclovine, used during a PET-CT scan, can help measure changes in tumor size in men with metastatic prostate cancer.

**Study Details:**

* Participants will undergo two 18 F-fluciclovine PET-CT scans, in addition to standard of care tests and procedures.
* The 18 F-fluciclovine PET-CT scans will involve an injection of the imaging agent into a vein, followed by a 30-minute scan.
* The study will take place at MD Anderson and will involve up to 45 participants.

**Risks and Side Effects:**

* Possible side effects of 18 F-fluciclovine include:
	+ Feeling of warmth or pain at the injection site
	+ Headache and dizziness
	+ Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, redness, blisters, itching, and/or local swellings)
	+ Anaphylactic shock (severe allergic reaction)
	+ Changes in the way you move or changes in your senses
* PET-CT scans may cause a feeling of being "closed in" during the scan, but the scanner is open at both ends and an intercom allows communication with doctors and staff.
* The PET-CT scan exposes the body to radiation, but the radioactive solution does not remain in the system for a long period of time.

**Benefits:**

* There are no direct benefits for participants in this study.
* Future patients may benefit from what is learned from this study.

**Voluntary Participation:**

* Participation is completely voluntary.
* Participants can choose not to take part in the study or withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

**Confidentiality:**

* MD Anderson will keep participant information confidential, but in some situations, the FDA may require the release of participant names.
* Participant information may be shared with federal agencies, the IRB, and study sponsors/supporters for research and safety-related purposes.

**Authorization:**

* By signing this consent form, participants authorize the use and disclosure of their protected health information (PHI) for research and safety-related purposes.
* Participants can withdraw their authorization in writing at any time.
