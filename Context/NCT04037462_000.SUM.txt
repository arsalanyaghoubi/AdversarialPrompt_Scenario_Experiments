**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary**

**Study Purpose:** The purpose of this study is to determine if giving a 7-13 day course of Dexamethasone (a steroid) should be a standard treatment when using immunotherapy to treat patients with non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). The study aims to see if Dexamethasone can improve the body's immune system, allowing immunotherapy to work better.

**Study Duration:** The study may last up to 6 years, including 1 year of treatment and 5 years of follow-up.

**Study Procedures:**

1. CT scans will be completed every 3 months as part of routine care.
2. Two FLT-PET scans will be completed at the University of Michigan to determine steroid uptake.
3. Extra tubes of blood will be collected at routine blood draws to analyze blood samples and correlate with treatment response.
4. A short course of Dexamethasone will be given before the first three immunotherapy infusions.

**Potential Benefits:**

* The study may help determine if Dexamethasone can improve the body's immune system, allowing immunotherapy to work better.
* The study may provide new information that can help others with the same condition.

**Potential Risks:**

* The treatment may not help, and the disease could progress.
* Dexamethasone may weaken the immune system, increasing the risk of infections.
* Common side effects of Dexamethasone include increased appetite, weight gain, and swelling.
* Common side effects of immunotherapy include inflammation of organs, rash, and shortness of breath.
* Rare side effects of immunotherapy include pyrexia, increased liver enzymes, and low red blood cell count.

**Confidentiality:** Personal information will be removed from the data collected, and a unique code will be assigned to link the data to the participant. The code will be kept on a secure drive located on the VA server.

**Costs and Reimbursement:**

* Participants will not be charged for any treatments or procedures that are part of the study.
* Participants will receive $25 per visit to the University of Michigan for the FLT-PET scan.

**Withdrawal:** Participants can withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits. Data already collected will be reviewed, but no further information will be collected.

**Right to Terminate Participation:** The investigator may terminate participation if the participant is not tolerating the treatment, has progressed, or it is no longer in their best interest.
