**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form**

This clinical trial, titled "Treat CEA positive rectal cancer with the Biological Product (CEA protein antigen plus BCG Vaccine) by the percutaneous use with the multiple puncture device," is designed to investigate the effectiveness of a new treatment for CEA positive rectal cancer. The study aims to assess the safety and efficacy of the Biological Product, which combines CEA protein antigen and BCG Vaccine, administered through a percutaneous route with a multiple puncture device.

**Key Information:**

* The study is a Phase 1 clinical trial, which means it is an initial, small-scale trial to assess the safety and efficacy of the treatment.
* The study will enroll 20 participants with CEA positive rectal cancer.
* The study duration is approximately 1 month, with follow-up visits at 1 and 3 months after the final study visit.
* The study involves percutaneous use of the Biological Product, as well as blood-drawing and other medical procedures.
* Participants will be asked to complete online questionnaires and report on their symptoms every day for 90 days.
* The study will collect medical information, data, results, and symptoms, which will be used to assess the safety and efficacy of the treatment.

**Risks and Benefits:**

* The potential risks associated with this study are minimal, primarily due to the collection of blood and other medical samples.
* The study will not benefit the participants directly, but the results may be used to guide future research, treatment, and prevention of CEA positive rectal cancer.
* Participants may experience side effects such as bruising, pain, and bleeding from blood-drawing or other medical procedures.

**Consent and Confidentiality:**

* Participants will be asked to sign and date a consent form, which will be reviewed and explained to them.
* The study records and samples will be kept confidential and labeled with a code number, not the participant's name or other personal information.
* The study will share participant information with local health authorities, regulatory agencies, and other researchers, but will not share personal identifying information.

**Withdrawal and Compensation:**

* Participants are free to leave the study at any time and for any reason.
* The study will not pay for treatment costs or provide compensation for injuries related to the study.
* Participants will receive a receipt document for each study visit completed.

**Contact Information:**

* Participants can contact the study site personnel, including Han Xu, M.D., Ph.D., FAPCR, Sponsor-Investigator, Medical Monitor, Safety Officer, Medical Director, IRB Chair, IORG Director, at 301-222-7143 or hanxumdphd@midinc.us.

**Biological Product:**

* The Biological Product is a combination of CEA protein antigen and BCG Vaccine, administered through a percutaneous route with a multiple puncture device.
* The product is prepared by adding 2 mL distilled water to 0.1 mg CEA protein antigen and mixing it with 1ml - 0.05 mg CEA.
* The Biological Product will be administered in the deltoid region, with the multiple puncture device used to puncture the skin and spread the biologics evenly.

**Method of Administration:**

* The Biological Product will be administered through a percutaneous route with a multiple puncture device, using a sterile, single-use device.
* The device consists of a plastic holder for a thin, wafer-like stainless steel plate from which 36 points protrude.
* The biologics will be spread evenly over the puncture area with the smooth edge of the device.

**Clinical Test:**

* Participants will undergo a tuberculin skin test and CEA protein skin test, with results to be read within 48 hours.
* The positive result is a skin test reading > 5 mm induration at 48 hours, while the negative result is a skin test reading < 5 mm induration at 48 hours.
