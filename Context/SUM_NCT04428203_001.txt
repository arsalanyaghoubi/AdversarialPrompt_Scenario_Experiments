**Study Title:** A phase I/Ib Study on the Safety of Epidiolex in Patients with Prostate Cancer with Rising PSA after Localized Therapy with either Surgery or Radiation

**Purpose:** The study aims to evaluate the safety and tolerability of Epidiolex (a CBD oil product) in patients with biochemically recurrent prostate cancer. The study will also assess whether Epidiolex can lower PSA and testosterone levels in these patients.

**Duration:** The study will last approximately 120 days, with a total of 18 hours of participation.

**Key Information:**

* You will be asked to take Epidiolex by mouth once a day for 120 days.
* You will have six study visits, each lasting approximately 3 hours.
* You will be asked to provide blood and urine samples at each visit.
* You will have standard scans to assess the status of your cancer.
* You will be asked to complete questionnaires about your health.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Common side effects include decreased appetite, diarrhea, fatigue, and rash.
* Rare but serious side effects include allergic reactions, blood sampling complications, and electrocardiogram (ECG) complications.
* You should not father a baby while on this study, as Epidiolex may involve risks to the embryo or fetus.

**Benefits:**

* You may benefit from taking part in this study by contributing to the understanding of Epidiolex's safety and efficacy in patients with biochemically recurrent prostate cancer.
* Your participation may lead to better treatments in the future.

**Confidentiality:**

* Your identifiable information will be kept confidential and protected by the University of Kentucky's Institutional Review Board.
* Your health information may be shared with other researchers, law enforcement agencies, and regulatory agencies.

**Withdrawal:**

* You can choose to withdraw from the study at any time.
* If you withdraw, the study drug will no longer be provided free of charge, and you may not be able to participate in another research study at the same time.

**HIPAA Authorization:**

* You will be asked to sign a HIPAA authorization form, which allows researchers to use and share your identifiable health information.
* You have the right to revoke the authorization at any time.

**Consent:**

* By signing this consent form, you are agreeing to participate in the study and understand the risks and benefits involved.
* You will receive a copy of this consent form after it has been signed.

Overall, the study aims to evaluate the safety and efficacy of Epidiolex in patients with biochemically recurrent prostate cancer. While there are potential risks and discomforts associated with the study, participants may benefit from contributing to the understanding of this treatment and potentially leading to better treatments in the future.
