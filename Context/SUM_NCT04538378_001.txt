**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form**

This consent form describes a Phase II clinical trial studying the combination of Olaparib (LYNPARZA) and Durvalumab (IMFINZI) in patients with EGFR-mutated adenocarcinomas that have transformed to Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC) and other Neuroendocrine Tumors.

**Key Information**

* The study is led by Principal Investigator Anish Thomas, M.D.
* The study aims to evaluate the efficacy of Olaparib and Durvalumab in shrinking tumors and improving symptoms in patients with SCLC and other Neuroendocrine Tumors.
* The study is being conducted at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) Clinical Center.
* Patients will receive Olaparib orally twice a day and Durvalumab via IV infusion every 4 weeks.
* The study will involve regular visits to the NIH Clinical Center for treatment and monitoring.
* Patients will be asked to provide blood and tumor samples for research purposes.
* The study will last for approximately 1-2 years, depending on the patient's response to treatment.

**Risks and Discomforts**

* The study drugs may cause side effects such as diarrhea, rash, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, upper respiratory tract infections, decreased appetite, shortness of breath, cough, and cough with or without mucus.
* Patients may experience serious side effects such as liver problems, inflammation of the lungs (pneumonitis), low thyroid (hypothyroidism), high thyroid (hyperthyroidism), kidney problems, nervous system problems, infusion-related reactions, inflammation of the intestine (colitis), and other serious side effects.
* Patients may be at risk for injury from the MRI magnet if they have certain types of metal in their body.
* Patients may experience psychological or social risks associated with the return of incidental or secondary findings, such as learning about genetic risks for other diseases or disabilities.

**Benefits**

* Patients may benefit from the study by shrinking their tumors or lessening their symptoms.
* The study may provide new information that will be helpful in understanding SCLC and other Neuroendocrine Tumors.
* The study may lead to the development of new treatments or therapies for SCLC and other Neuroendocrine Tumors.

**Confidentiality Protections**

* The NIH will do its best to keep patients' personal information private.
* The NIH will not release identifiable information collected about patients without their written permission.
* The NIH has received a Certificate of Confidentiality to protect patients' information.

**Informed Consent**

* Patients are free to stop taking part in the study at any time.
* Patients may choose not to participate in the study or withdraw from the study at any time.
* Patients will be informed about any new information that may affect their choice to participate in the study.

**Storage and Sharing of Specimens and Data**

* Patients' specimens and data will be stored and used for future research studies.
* Patients' genomic data will be shared with other researchers and placed in a large database for broad sharing with the research community.
* Patients' data will be labeled with a code and not with their name or other information that could be used to easily identify them.

**Compensation, Reimbursement, and Payment**

* Patients will not receive compensation for participation in the study.
* The NIH will cover the cost of some patients' expenses, such as travel and lodging.
* Patients may have to pay for tests and procedures performed outside the NIH Clinical Center if they are not covered by their insurance company.

**Clinical Trial Registration and Results Reporting**

* A description of the study will be available on ClinicalTrials.gov.
* The study will be registered on ClinicalTrials.gov, and the results will be reported on the website.
