**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Name:** Adaptive-Dose to Mediastinum with Immunotherapy (Durvalumab MEDI4736) and Radiation in Locally-advanced non-small cell lung cancer (ADMIRAL Trial)

**Objective:** To improve cancer control in stage III non-small cell lung cancer patients by using a combination of immunotherapy (durvalumab), chemotherapy, and radiation.

**Study Design:** This is a clinical trial that involves approximately 60 clinic visits over 2 years. Participants will receive chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and durvalumab (a type of immunotherapy) for 2 years.

**Risks:**

* Side effects of durvalumab, such as hepatitis, pneumonitis, and interstitial lung disease
* Radiation risks, including fatigue and pneumonitis
* Potential for increased toxicity due to the combination of immunotherapy and radiation
* Unknown risks to an embryo, fetus, or nursing infant
* Reproductive risks, including the potential for birth defects or infertility

**Benefits:**

* Potential for improved cancer control and survival
* Opportunity to contribute to the advancement of medical knowledge and treatment options for stage III non-small cell lung cancer patients

**Inclusion Criteria:**

* Patients with stage III non-small cell lung cancer
* Patients who are receiving standard of care treatment for stage III non-small cell lung cancer

**Exclusion Criteria:**

* Patients who are pregnant or breastfeeding
* Patients who have a history of certain medical conditions, such as liver disease or kidney disease
* Patients who are taking certain medications that may interact with durvalumab or radiation therapy

**Study Procedures:**

* Participants will undergo imaging appointments, daily radiation for 6 weeks with weekly chemotherapy, and immunotherapy once every 4 weeks for 2 years
* Participants will undergo research blood draws, which may include optional PET/CT scans and repeat lung tumor biopsies
* Participants will be followed for approximately 2 years, with regular clinic visits and monitoring of side effects

**Consent Form:**

* Participants must sign a consent form to participate in the study
* The consent form includes information about the study, the risks and benefits, and the participant's rights and responsibilities
* Participants have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits

**Optional Procedures:**

* Participants may be asked to donate their tumor tissue for research purposes
* Participants may be asked to undergo a research biopsy
* Participants may be asked to undergo a PET/CT scan instead of the standard CT scan and restaging CT scan
