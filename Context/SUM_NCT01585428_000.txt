**Summary of Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Title:** A Phase II Study of Lymphodepletion followed by Autologous Tumor-Infiltrating Lymphocytes and High-Dose Aldesleukin for Human Papillomavirus-Associated Cancers

**Objective:** To evaluate the efficacy and safety of a new experimental treatment for human papillomavirus (HPV)-associated cancers.

**Eligibility Criteria:**

* Patients with HPV-associated cancers such as cervical, vulvar, vaginal, penile, anal, or oropharyngeal cancer.
* Patients who have not received any previous treatment for their cancer.

**Study Design:**

* The study involves several stages:
	+ Work up (1-2 weeks): Patients undergo various tests, including imaging procedures, heart and lung function tests, and laboratory tests.
	+ Chemotherapy (1 week): Patients receive two chemotherapy drugs to temporarily suppress their immune system.
	+ Cell infusion and aldesleukin regimen (1-5 days): Patients receive the autologous tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes (TIL) cells and high-dose aldesleukin.
	+ Recovery (1-2 weeks): Patients recover from the effects of treatment.
	+ Follow-up: Patients return to the clinic for physical exams, review of side effects, labs, and scans every 1-6 months.

**Potential Risks and Discomforts:**

* Long-term decrease in immune function.
* Loss of fertility.
* Death.
* Side effects of chemotherapy and aldesleukin, including fever, chills, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and skin rash.
* Potential autoimmune disease.
* Bowel perforation.
* Heart damage.
* Kidney damage.

**Potential Benefits:**

* Shrinkage of tumors.
* Lessening of symptoms, such as pain.

**Confidentiality:**

* The NIH will do its best to keep personal information private.
* However, the NIH may release information to organizations involved in research, quality assurance, and data analysis.

**Payment:**

* Patients will not be paid for taking part in the study.
* Reimbursement of travel and subsistence will be consistent with NIH guidelines.
