**Summary of Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Study Title:** A Pilot Study of 18 F-DCFBC PET/CT in Prostate Cancer

**Objective:** To determine if the experimental radiotracer, 18 F-DCFBC, can identify sites of prostate cancer in the body.

**Eligibility Criteria:**

* Adult men with newly diagnosed, relapsed, or metastatic prostate cancer
* Up to 125 men will be included in the study

**Study Design:**

* Three groups: patients with cancer only in the prostate, patients with rising PSA without evidence of disease, and patients with verified metastatic disease
* Imaging studies will be performed using 18 F-DCFBC PET/CT and MRI (for group 1 only)
* Additional PET/CT scan with 18 F-NaF will be performed for patients in group 3 to evaluate bone disease

**Procedures:**

* Screening labs and physical exam
* Imaging studies (MRI and PET/CT)
* Urinary catheter insertion (for group 2 only)
* Prostatectomy (for group 1 only)
* Follow-up period of one year to collect information related to prostate cancer

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Risks of MRI (claustrophobia, noise, and potential side effects from Gadolinium)
* Risks from IV and injection of radiotracer (pain, bruising, and potential side effects)
* Risks of urinary catheter insertion (bleeding, infection, and potential side effects)
* Radiation exposure (small amount of radiation exposure from PET/CT scans)

**Benefits:**

* No direct benefits, but the knowledge gained from this study may help others in the future who have cancer

**Confidentiality and Privacy:**

* The NIH will do its best to keep your personal information private
* However, there may be situations where your information is shared with other organizations or individuals

**Withdrawal from the Study:**

* You can stop taking part in the study at any time
* If you decide to withdraw, your samples and data will be destroyed upon request

**Conflict of Interest:**

* Members of the research team may have a conflict of interest due to their financial relationships with companies that make products used in the study

**Other Pertinent Information:**

* Confidentiality and release of information
* Policy regarding research-related injuries
* Payments to research volunteers
* Problems or questions about the study
