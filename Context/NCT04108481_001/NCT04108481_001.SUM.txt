**Project Title:** Immunotherapy combined with Yttrium-90 RadioEmbolization in the treatment of Colorectal Cancer with Liver Metastases

**Summary:**

This is a clinical trial to study the combination of immunotherapy and Yttrium-90 RadioEmbolization (Y90-RE) for the treatment of colorectal cancer with liver metastases. The immunotherapy drug, durvalumab, is still experimental and not approved by the FDA for this specific cancer type. The study aims to assess the safety of this combination treatment and determine if it can help fight cancer.

**Key Points:**

* Approximately 18 people will participate in the study.
* The study will last for approximately 2 years.
* Participants will receive radioembolization and durvalumab in combination.
* Radioembolization will be given one time during the study treatment.
* Durvalumab will be administered through IV infusion, and the number of infusions will range from 1 to 5.
* Participants will undergo regular clinic visits, blood draws, and imaging studies.
* Tissue biopsy will be performed at the end of treatment.
* Participants' tissue and blood will be stored for future research studies.

**Risks and Benefits:**

* Risks: Side effects of immunotherapy and radioembolization, including skin changes, hair loss, and delayed wound healing.
* Benefits: Potential improvement in cancer treatment outcomes and contribution to future research studies.

**Voluntary Participation:**

* Participation in the study is completely voluntary.
* Participants can stop participating at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

**Confidentiality:**

* The study team will keep participant information confidential to the extent permitted by law.
* Participants' health information may be shared with other parties, including regulatory agencies and study sponsors.

**Informed Consent:**

* Participants will receive a copy of this informed consent document.
* Participants must sign the document to indicate their understanding and agreement to participate in the study.
