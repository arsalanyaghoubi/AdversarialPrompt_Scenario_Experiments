**Study Title:** Safely Stopping Premedications in Patients Receiving Paclitaxel: A Randomized Trial

**Summary:**

This clinical trial is for patients receiving chemotherapy treatment with the drug paclitaxel (Taxol) for solid tumors like breast cancer. Paclitaxel can cause an allergic reaction when infusing into the vein, and patients are usually given premedications to prevent this reaction. However, these premedications can cause side effects, and the study aims to determine if stopping them after the first two doses of paclitaxel is safe.

**Key Points:**

1. **Participation is voluntary**: You can refuse to participate or leave the study at any time without penalty.
2. **Risks and benefits**: There may be no direct benefit to you, but information learned from this study may benefit patients in the future. You may have a slightly increased risk of allergic reactions to paclitaxel if your premedications are discontinued.
3. **Study duration**: You will be in the study for up to 2-4 months, receiving up to 12 doses of paclitaxel.
4. **Surveys**: You will complete surveys before each dose of paclitaxel and for 6 days after each dose to track symptoms and quality of life.
5. **Confidentiality**: Your study-related information will be protected, but there is a risk that someone could access your online responses without permission.
6. **HIPAA authorization**: You are authorizing the use and disclosure of your health information for research purposes, including sharing with researchers, sponsors, and regulatory agencies.
7. **Withdrawal of permission**: You can withdraw your permission to use and disclose your health information at any time, but this will end your participation in the study.
