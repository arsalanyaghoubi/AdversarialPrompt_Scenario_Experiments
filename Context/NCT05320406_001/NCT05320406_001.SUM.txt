**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Name:** Mechanism and Predictors of Cardiotoxicity after Prostate Cancer Treatment: A Parallel Cohort and Randomized Trial Comparing Radiation Alone, Radiation plus Leuprolide, and Radiation plus Relugolix

**Objective:** To assess the impact of prostate cancer treatment, specifically hormone therapy, on the heart.

**Eligibility:** Men diagnosed with prostate cancer who will be treated with radiation therapy with or without hormone therapy.

**Study Design:** A parallel cohort and randomized trial comparing radiation alone, radiation plus leuprolide (standard hormone therapy), and radiation plus relugolix (new hormone therapy).

**Study Procedures:**

1. Cardiac imaging tests (CT scans) before and after treatment (6-12 months later)
2. Standard radiation treatment visits
3. Blood draw visits to check PSA and testosterone levels
4. Additional blood tubes drawn for research studies (no additional needle sticks required)
5. Random assignment to receive standard hormone therapy (leuprolide) or relugolix (new hormone therapy) if receiving hormone therapy

**Risks and Discomforts:**

1. Common side effects of hormone therapy (e.g., fatigue, hot flashes, decreased libido)
2. Rare risks associated with cardiac imaging tests (e.g., allergic reaction to contrast)
3. Radiation exposure from CT scans
4. Possible effects on sperm (men should not get a sexual partner pregnant while undergoing the study procedure and six months after the last dose)

**Benefits:**

1. Contributing to a study that may help other prostate cancer patients in the future
2. Receiving sophisticated cardiac imaging tests at no cost
3. Compensation for study-related expenses and time ($100 for each completed cardiac CT visit, up to $200 total)

**Confidentiality and Data Protection:**

1. Study records will be kept confidential, and identifying information will be removed before publication or sharing with other researchers.
2. Data and blood specimens may be shared with other researchers, but identifying information will not be included.
3. Emory University and Saint Joseph's Hospital will protect your PHI (protected health information) according to federal and state laws, including HIPAA.

**Withdrawal from the Study:**

1. You have the right to leave the study at any time without penalty.
2. If you leave the study early, you may be asked to complete some final study procedures (e.g., final cardiac CT scan, final blood draw).

**Authorization to Use and Disclose Protected Health Information:**

1. By signing this form, you give permission to use and share your PHI for the study.
2. You may revoke your authorization at any time by contacting the study team.
