This clinical trial consent form outlines a Phase I/II trial of an HPV vaccine (PRGN-2009) alone or in combination with an anti-PD-L1/TGF-β trap (M7824) in patients with HPV-associated cancers. The study aims to determine a safe dose of the HPV vaccine and the combination therapy, as well as to evaluate its efficacy in shrinking tumors.

**Key Information:**

* The study consists of two phases: Phase I and Phase II.
* Participants will be randomly assigned to receive either the HPV vaccine alone (Group A) or in combination with M7824 (Group B).
* The HPV vaccine will be administered via injection under the skin, while M7824 will be administered via intravenous infusion.
* Participants will undergo regular assessments, including physical exams, laboratory tests, and imaging studies, to evaluate their response to treatment and potential side effects.
* The study will last for approximately one year, with follow-up visits and long-term follow-up every year for the rest of the participant's life.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Common side effects of the HPV vaccine may include injection site reactions, fever, flu-like symptoms, and skin rash.
* Common side effects of M7824 may include fatigue, diarrhea, nausea, constipation, and swelling of the lower legs or hands.
* Serious side effects of M7824 may include bleeding, anemia, thrombocytopenia, and inflammation of the lungs, liver, or kidneys.
* Participants may experience allergic reactions or reactions to the infusions, which can be mild to severe.
* The study also involves the use of CT scans and CT-guided biopsies, which may expose participants to radiation.

**Benefits:**

* Participants may benefit from the study by receiving a new and potentially more effective treatment for their HPV-associated cancer.
* The study may also provide valuable information for future research and the development of new treatments.

**Confidentiality and Data Sharing:**

* The NIH will store and use participant specimens and data for future research studies, including studies that may be conducted at the NIH or other institutions.
* Participant information will be kept confidential, and identifiers will be removed from specimens and data before sharing with other researchers.
* The NIH will also share participant genomic data in a large database for broad sharing with the research community.

**Consent:**

* Participants must provide informed consent before enrolling in the study.
* Participants have the right to withdraw from the study at any time and for any reason.
* Participants will be asked to sign a consent document and provide their initials to confirm their agreement to participate in the study.
