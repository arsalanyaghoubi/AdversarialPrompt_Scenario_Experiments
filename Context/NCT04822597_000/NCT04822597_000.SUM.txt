**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary:**

**Study Title:** Optimizing Topical Pain Control for Breast Cancer Patients Undergoing Preoperative Radiotracer Injection for Sentinel Lymph Node Mapping

**Objective:** To compare four different pain treatments for the injection of radioactive tracer in breast cancer patients undergoing sentinel lymph node biopsies.

**Inclusion Criteria:** Women with breast cancer who are scheduled to undergo sentinel lymph node biopsy with a standard injection of radioactive tracer before surgery.

**Exclusion Criteria:** Pregnant women, individuals under 18 years old, those with an allergy to lidocaine or other topical anesthetics, and individuals with a medical contraindication to lidocaine or other topical anesthetics.

**Study Groups:** 180 participants will be randomly assigned to one of four study groups:

1. Ice on the breast (usual treatment)
2. Lidocaine patch on the breast
3. A vibrating distraction device (Buzzy) and ice on the breast
4. Lidocaine patch on the breast followed by a vibrating distraction device (Buzzy) and ice on the breast

**Participation:** Participants will receive their assigned pain treatment, followed by the injection of radioactive tracer. After the injection, they will complete a short survey about their experience. Participation will last approximately 20 minutes.

**Confidentiality and Data Protection:** The study team will protect participant confidentiality and data, and will only share information with authorized personnel. Participants' names will be replaced with numbers to maintain anonymity.

**Benefits and Risks:** There are no direct benefits to participation, but participants may experience improved pain management. There is a small risk of skin reactions or allergic reactions to the pain treatment.

**Authorization:** Participants must sign and date this consent form, and verbally agree to participate in the study.

**Withdrawal:** Participants can withdraw from the study at any time, without penalty or loss of medical care.

**Contact:** Participants can contact the Principal Investigator, Dr. Heather Neuman, or UW Health Patient Relations with questions or concerns.
