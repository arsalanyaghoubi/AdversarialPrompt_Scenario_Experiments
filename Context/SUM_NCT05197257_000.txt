**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary:**

**Study Title:** 68 Ga-PSMA-11 PET in Patients with Prostate Cancer

**Objective:** To provide clinical access to the prostate cancer imaging agent 68Ga-PSMA-11 for positron emission tomography (PET) imaging in patients with prostate cancer at the University of Colorado.

**Eligibility:** Up to 100 patients from the area will participate in the study. Several thousand patients with prostate cancer have already received this 68Ga-PSMA-11 PET imaging agent with no reported serious adverse events.

**Study Procedure:**

1. 68Ga-PSMA-11 PET/CT or PET/MRI scan will be performed, depending on the patient's specific situation.
2. The study will last approximately 2 hours or less.
3. An intravenous catheter will be inserted, and 37mCi of 68Ga-PSMA-11 will be administered through the catheter over less than 30 seconds.
4. A waiting period (uptake time) of approximately 60 minutes will occur before scanning.
5. The scan will be performed, which will take approximately 30-45 minutes.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

1. Risks of Insertion of the intravenous catheter:
	* Pain when inserting the tube into the vein
	* Redness, swelling, or bruising where the tube goes under the skin
	* Infection or blood clot in the vein (rare)
2. Risks of Computed Tomography (CT):
	* Exposure to radiation, which increases the risk of developing cancer
	* No evidence of serious adverse events for diagnostic procedures
3. Risks of Having an MRI:
	* Claustrophobia, discomfort, or anxiety when lying inside the scanner
	* Allergic reactions, itching, and rash (rare)
	* Kidney damage (rare)
4. Risks of 68 Ga-PSMA-11 Positron Emission Tomography (PET):
	* Exposure to radiation, which increases the risk of developing cancer
	* Claustrophobia, discomfort, or anxiety when lying inside the scanner
	* Allergic reactions, itching, and rash (rare)

**Benefits:**

1. The results of the 68Ga-PSMA-11 PET study will be available to the patient's doctors to use for their care.
2. The study is designed to provide 68Ga PSMA-11 PET imaging information for patients with prostate cancer.

**Voluntary Participation:**

1. Participation in the study is voluntary.
2. Patients have the right to choose not to participate or to stop at any time.
3. If patients refuse or decide to withdraw later, they will not lose any benefits or rights to which they are entitled.

**Confidentiality:**

1. The University of Colorado Denver | Anschutz Medical Campus and its affiliated health systems have rules to protect information about patients.
2. Federal and state laws, including HIPAA, also protect patient privacy.
3. Confidentiality cannot be guaranteed.

**Data and Specimens:**

1. The data collected from patients during the study are important to the study and to future research.
2. If patients join the study, the data given by them to the investigators for this research no longer belong to them.
3. The University or the health systems involved in the study may use the data for future research only with patient consent or Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval.

**Consent Form:**

1. Patients must sign and date the consent form to participate in the study.
2. The consent form explains the possible risks and benefits of the study.
3. Patients have the right to request access to their personal health information from the Investigator.
