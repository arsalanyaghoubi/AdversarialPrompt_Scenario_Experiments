**Clinical Trial Consent Form Summary**

This clinical trial consent form outlines the details of a Phase II study titled "A Phase II Study of Niraparib in Combination with EGFR Inhibitor Panitumumab in patients with advanced colorectal cancer." The study aims to evaluate the safety and efficacy of combining Niraparib and Panitumumab in patients with advanced colon and rectal cancers.

**Study Details:**

* The study is led by Principal Investigator Olatunji B. Alese, MD, and supported by Tesaro (now owned by GlaxoSmithKline).
* The study will enroll approximately 26 patients at Emory University.
* Participants will receive Niraparib pills once a day and Panitumumab intravenously every 2 weeks.
* The study duration is expected to last at least 8 weeks, but participants may continue in the study as long as their doctor feels they are benefiting from the therapy.
* Participants will undergo regular study visits, including blood tests, physical examinations, and imaging studies.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Common side effects of Niraparib include:
	+ Decrease in blood platelets, red blood cells, and white blood cells
	+ High blood pressure
	+ Fatigue, weakness, and lack of energy
	+ Nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea
	+ Joint pain, back pain, and stomach pain
* Rare but serious side effects of Niraparib include:
	+ Severe life-threatening infection due to low white cell counts
	+ Severe increase in blood pressure
	+ Brain condition with symptoms including seizures, headache, confusion, and changes in vision
* Participants may experience skin punch biopsy-related side effects, including pain, bruising, and infection.

**Benefits:**

* The study is not designed to benefit participants directly, but the results may be used to help others in the future.
* Participants will not receive compensation for their time and effort.

**Confidentiality and Data Protection:**

* The study team will follow federal and state privacy laws, including HIPAA, to protect participants' confidential information.
* Participants' identifiable information will be removed or coded to protect their anonymity.
* De-identified data from the study may be shared with the research community to advance science and health.

**Withdrawal from the Study:**

* Participants have the right to leave the study at any time without penalty.
* Researchers may ask participants to complete final study procedures before withdrawing from the study.

**Contact Information:**

* Participants can contact Dr. Olatunji Alese, MD, at Emory University for questions, concerns, or complaints about the research.
* Participants can also contact the Emory Institutional Review Board for further information.

**Additional Studies:**

* Participants may be eligible to participate in additional studies, which will be explained separately.
