**Summary of Clinical Trial Consent Form**

**Purpose:**

The purpose of this study is to evaluate a new radiation technique called "image-guided bone marrow-sparing intensity modulated radiation therapy (IG-BMS-IMRT)" for treating cervical cancer. This study aims to determine if IG-BMS-IMRT reduces side effects and improves quality of life while maintaining equal cancer control compared to standard radiation techniques.

**Duration:**

The expected duration of this study is approximately 5 years.

**Procedures:**

* Imaging studies (CT, MRI, PET/CT)
* Standard treatment for cervical cancer (chemotherapy and radiation therapy)
* IG-BMS-IMRT radiation therapy
* Blood samples for laboratory testing
* Evaluation of side effects and quality of life
* Follow-up visits at 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, 3-4 months, 6, 12, 24, and 36 months after treatment completion

**Risks:**

* Side effects from radiation therapy (redness, skin irritation, loss of pubic hair, tiredness, nausea, sterility, etc.)
* Side effects from chemotherapy (kidney toxicity, ringing in the ears, hearing loss, bone marrow suppression, etc.)
* Risks associated with blood draws (discomfort, pain, bleeding, swelling, etc.)
* Risks associated with IV contrast (allergic reactions, swelling of the heart, cramps of the voicebox, etc.)
* Risks associated with FDG-PET/CT scans (allergic reactions, discomfort, bruising, bleeding, fainting, etc.)
* Risks associated with MRI scans (claustrophobia, anxiety, discomfort, tiredness, etc.)

**Benefits:**

* Direct medical benefit to the participant (reduced side effects and improved quality of life)
* Contribution to medical knowledge and potential benefits for future patients

**Alternatives:**

* Standard treatment for cervical cancer (chemotherapy and radiation therapy)
* Participation in other research studies
* Supportive care only (no cancer treatment)

**Compensation:**

* No payment to participants for participating in the study
* Optional procedures (MRI scans) may be compensated ($100 per visit)

**Confidentiality:**

* Research records will be maintained confidentially
* Data obtained from the study may be published or given to regulatory authorities

**Voluntary Participation:**

* Participation in the study is entirely voluntary
* Participants may withdraw their consent to participate at any time without affecting their regular treatments or medical care

**Optional Procedures:**

* Participants may be asked to participate in FLT-PET and/or functional MRI optional procedures
* These procedures may involve injection of a radioactive substance and exposure to a small amount of radiation
* Participants may give permission to take part in these optional procedures or decline.
