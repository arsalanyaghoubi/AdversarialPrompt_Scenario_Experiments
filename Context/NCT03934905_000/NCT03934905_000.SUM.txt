Here's a clear and concise summary of the clinical trial consent form:

**Study Title:** Phase II trial of the effect of the Nutritional Supplement Sulforaphane on Doxorubicin-Associated Cardiac Dysfunction.

**Purpose:** To investigate whether taking a nutritional supplement called sulforaphane (SFN) can help protect the heart from damage caused by doxorubicin (DOX) chemotherapy in patients with breast cancer.

**Eligibility:** 60 women with breast cancer who will receive DOX chemotherapy are invited to participate.

**Duration:** The study will last for 1 year.

**What to Expect:**

1. **Screening Visit:** Review of the consent form, review of cancer status, echocardiogram (ECHO), electrocardiogram (EKG), and pregnancy test (if applicable).
2. **Baseline Visit:** Randomization into either the SFN or placebo group, distribution of pills, completion of a health questionnaire, and blood draw.
3. **DOX Infusions:** Blood draws, pill distribution, and weighing.
4. **Follow-up Visits:** Blood draws, ECHO, PET CT scans, and health questionnaires at 1 week and 1 year after the last DOX infusion.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Side effects of sulforaphane (vomiting, abdominal pain, constipation, increased appetite)
* Risks associated with blood draws (pain, discomfort, redness, infection, bleeding, bruising, and swelling)

**Benefits:** None guaranteed, but participants who receive SFN may benefit from the supplement.

**Confidentiality and Privacy:** The study will keep participants' involvement confidential to the extent permitted by law.

**Funding:** The study is funded by the NIH, DoD, and internal funding from the Department of Internal Medicine.

**Costs:** Participants will be responsible for costs related to standard care, including blood work and ECHO/EKG. If insurance denies coverage, the study will pay for it.

**Compensation:** None.

**Withdrawal:** Participants may leave the study at any time, but they will not be able to remove any information collected up to that point.

**Contact:** For questions about the study, contact Dr. Sharda Singh at 806-743-1540.

**Authorization to Use and/or Disclose Protected Health Information:** Participants will be asked to sign a separate form authorizing the use and/or disclosure of their protected health information for research purposes.
