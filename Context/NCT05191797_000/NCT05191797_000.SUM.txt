**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

**Study Purpose:** This is a Phase I/II clinical trial studying the safety and efficacy of an investigational drug called bomedemstat in combination with atezolizumab, an immunotherapy, for patients with newly diagnosed extensive-stage small cell lung cancer (ES-SCLC).

**Study Duration:** The study is expected to last 24 months, and participants will be followed for up to 5 years after the study ends.

**Study Procedures:**

* Participants will receive bomedemstat capsules daily and atezolizumab on Day 1 of every cycle.
* Participants will undergo regular physical examinations, vital sign measurements, blood draws, and tumor assessments (CT scans every 8 weeks).
* Participants will also undergo screening tests, including a physical examination, vital sign measurements, and blood draws, up to 28 days prior to starting study treatment.

**Risks and Side Effects:**

* Bomedemstat may cause side effects such as low platelet count, nausea, diarrhea, altered taste, low red blood cell level, vomiting, fatigue, and others.
* Atezolizumab may cause side effects such as fatigue, joint pain, lack of energy, decreased appetite, diarrhea, urinary tract infection, shortness of breath, cough, itching of the skin, nausea, fever, rash, vomiting, muscle and bone pain, and others.
* Rare but serious side effects may include fever with low white blood cell count, gastrointestinal bleeding, low platelet count or worsened low platelet count, bruising, irregular heart rhythm, bleeding in the lungs, worsening lung function, painful spleen, differentiation syndrome, heart block, constipation, heart failure, fungal infection in the chest, illness with fever, headache, rectal bleeding, respiratory failure, and others.

**Benefits:**

* Participants may or may not receive any benefit from this investigational study.
* The study may help future patients with ES-SCLC.

**Confidentiality and Privacy:**

* The study will collect participants' medical and health records, which will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law.
* The study will use a Certificate of Confidentiality to protect participants' identifying information.
* Participants' genetic information may be shared with other researchers for future research.

**Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal:**

* Participation in the study is voluntary, and participants can withdraw at any time without penalty.
* Participants can stop taking the study medication at any time and continue with their regular medical care.

**Genetic Information Protection:**

* The Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA) helps protect genetic information about participants.
* GINA restricts access to genetic information so that it cannot be used for health insurance coverage decisions.
