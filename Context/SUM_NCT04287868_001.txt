**Summary of the Clinical Trial Consent Form:**

This consent form describes a Phase I/II clinical trial studying the effectiveness of a combination immunotherapy (PDS0101, M7824, and NHS-IL12) in treating advanced HPV-associated malignancies, such as cervical, oropharyngeal, anal, vulvar, vaginal, penile, and squamous cell rectal cancers. The trial aims to determine if this combination is effective in shrinking tumors and improving patient outcomes.

**Key Information:**

* The trial is being conducted at the NIH Clinical Center.
* Eligible participants must have been diagnosed with advanced HPV-associated cancer.
* The trial involves administering a combination of three immunotherapy drugs: PDS0101, M7824, and NHS-IL12.
* Participants will receive regular assessments, including physical exams, laboratory tests, and imaging scans, to monitor their response to treatment and potential side effects.
* Participants may experience side effects, including fatigue, nausea, diarrhea, chills, anemia, increased blood sugar, flu-like symptoms, and others.
* The trial will last for approximately one year, with participants receiving up to 8 doses of PDS0101, 26 doses of M7824, and 13 doses of NHS-IL12.
* Participants will be followed for long-term outcomes, including disease progression, survival, and potential retreatment.

**Risks and Discomforts:**

* Potential side effects of the study treatment, including allergic reactions, infusion-related reactions, and immune-mediated side effects.
* Risks related to blood collection, including pain, bruising, and low red blood cell count.
* Risks related to tumor biopsies, including brief discomfort, bruising, and rare complications such as infection or bleeding.
* Risks related to pregnancy, including unknown risks to the fetus or unborn child.
* Risks related to radiation exposure from CT scans and biopsies, including increased risk of cancer.

**Benefits:**

* Potential benefits of the study treatment, including reduction in tumor size and improvement in symptoms and/or survival.
* Potential benefits to others who may benefit from the knowledge gained from this trial in the future.

**Options:**

* Participants can choose not to participate in the trial and pursue other treatment options, such as surgery, radiation, or approved therapies.
* Participants can withdraw from the trial at any time and will not lose any benefits to which they are otherwise entitled.

**Storage, Sharing, and Future Research:**

* Participants' specimens and data may be stored and used for future research studies, including studies that may lead to the development of commercial products.
* Participants' genomic data may be shared with the research community through data repositories.
* Participants' medical information may be shared with researchers, government agencies, and other organizations for research, quality assurance, and data analysis.

**Confidentiality Protections:**

* The NIH has received a Certificate of Confidentiality to protect participants' identifying information.
* The NIH will not release identifiable information without participants' written permission, except in certain circumstances.

**Compensation, Reimbursement, and Payment:**

* Participants will not receive compensation for participation in the study.
* The NIH will cover some expenses related to the study, including travel and lodging costs.
